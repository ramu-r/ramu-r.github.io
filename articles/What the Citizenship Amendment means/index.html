What the Citizenship Amendment means

The Citizenship amendment says that people from Afghanistan, Bangladesh or Pakistan who illegally crossed the border to India can become a citizen if they are a Hindu, Sikh, Buddhist, Jain, Parsi or a Christian. In other words everyone who crossed the border, except Muslims, can become citizens.

Even if it is outright discrimination, it by itself isn’t very harmful. At most it can only prevent migrants from settling in India and won’t affect people who are already living in India. But it becomes a powerful weapon for discrimination when combined along with the NRC process.

The NRC is a national register, a list of citizens, for Assam. It exists only for Assam due to the history of migration and cross border issues in that state. Now the government has started verifying the documents of Assamese to identify the illegal migrants. This should lead to everyone who aren’t Indians being identified and sent back to their own countries, hopefully. But because of the citizenship amendment, those who aren’t muslims will be given citizenship and allowed to stay in India while only the Muslims will be sent back

The cases of illegal migrants are decided by the foreigner’s tribunals which have lawyers and civil servants with as little as seven years’ experience as judges, and all the qualification they need is a two days’ orientation course. The appointment is done by the state government and the judges have no security againt transfer or removal, all of this leading to the tribunals working as merely an arm of the BJP state government in Assam.

And this isn’t just about Assam anymore as many BJP leaders have already started demanding for NRC to be created for the whole country. Many people, especially the poor who were born in India but have no documents will be affected. But It’s only the Muslims who are going to be identified and deported because people of other religions will be allowed to become citizens as said in the citizenship amendment.

In effect the NRC, together with the citizenship amendment, becomes an instrument for targetting only the Muslims and deporting them. As said many times before, and as said about many of BJP’s actions, this goes against the values of India, ideals of our constitution, against our basic human decency to treat others as humans.
