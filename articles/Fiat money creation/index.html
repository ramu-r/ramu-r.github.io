Fiat money creation in an economy

The money creation process in an economy is a cycle of estimating future productive work, creating money for that level of activity and then checking if the estimate was wrong and adjusting the next cycle accordingly.

In practice, The money creation step is done by the central bank through the commercial banks and the central banks only manages the whole process.

Money creation

Banks create Fiat Money through debts. The banks can give out more loans than they have deposits, which creates fiat money. It is called fiat because it is paper money without anything like gold to back it. Any value is based on estimates of future productivity than existing value. The existance of future productivity is ensured by banks as they give loans only to viable projects, not for flights of fantasy. This is the money creation step.

As the loan amount is spend by the individual, it leads to a small dose of inflation because now there’s more money without value. Say it is spend to create a factory or workshop. When this productive work finishes, it would have created some new value to make up for the increase in the new money in the economy thus bringing down that small dose of inflation.

What if the the money is not spend on any productive enterprise? It would cause the small dose of inflation to persist and when large amounts of the economy as a whole indugle in wasteful enterprise, it would lead to largescale noticeable inflation. It is the central bank’s job to ensure it doesn’t come to that.

Adjusting future money creation

The central bank checks if the money is being spent on productive work or not, so that it can limit future money creation. This is done through the rate of inflation. If banks allowed individuals to keep on taking new loans and spending it on booze, it would lead to more money than production, leading to higher inflation rates. If the inflation rate in the economy as a whole is higher, the central banks knows more money is being created than productive work in the economy.

The central banks controls the money supply by adjusting the bank rate, repo rate, CRR and similar measures. In a scenario of high inflation, the central bank turns these levers to reduce the loan creation in the economy thus reducing creation of money.
